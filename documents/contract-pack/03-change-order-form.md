## HOW TO USE THIS DOCUMENT

A change order documents scope changes after a project is underway. It protects both parties: the client knows exactly what the added work costs before it starts, and you have written authorization before you spend time on work outside the original contract.

The rule: never do out-of-scope work without a signed Change Order. No exceptions.

Fill every `[BLANK]`. Delete annotation text before sending. Attach this to the original Freelance Services Agreement — it becomes a binding amendment.

> **The Most Important Thing About Change Orders**
>
> The conversation before the signature is the work. When a client asks for something new, your response is: "That's outside our original scope — let me write up a Change Order." Say it every time, without apology. Clients who are used to working with professionals will expect it. Clients who push back are telling you something important about how they'll behave on the next project.
>
> Never do the work first and then send the Change Order. By the time the client sees the work, they've lost the urgency to approve new spend. The form goes first. The work starts after it's signed.
