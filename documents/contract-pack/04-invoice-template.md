> **Invoice Best Practices**
>
> **Send the invoice the day the work is done.** Not three weeks later. Every day you wait is a day you've given the client a free loan.
>
> **Follow up at 7 days.** A short, friendly email: "Just checking in on Invoice #INV-001 — wanted to make sure it was received." This is not aggressive. It is professional. Accounting teams are busy and invoices get lost.
>
> **Follow up at 15 days (past due).** This time, reference the late fee clause. You don't have to be rude — just factual. "Invoice #INV-001 is now past due. Per our agreement, late fees of [X]% begin accruing from today. Please advise on payment status."
>
> **Get a client's billing contact before the project starts.** The person who hired you is often not the person who processes payments. Ask: "Who should I send invoices to, and do you have a purchase order number I should reference?" Asking this before the project starts makes you look professional. Asking it after makes you look like an afterthought.
